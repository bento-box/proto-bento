Opening Our Doors

Fri Apr 21, 2017 by Abhijay Eshwar

Welcome to our Kitchen

Bento-Box is made up of a handful of nerds, but as we open up to the public, we want to make a promise to our users: to keep you updated on what we’re cooking up behind the scenes at Bento-Box without too much confusing tech jargon. Bento-Box is a small organization made up of students of Computer Science, and we’re already having tons of fun working on our projects together. We want to share our experience with our userbase and excite you all with the fun things we’re building for you at bento. It’s a personal choice of ours to put lots of focus on our users. Oneof our highest priorities is and will always be taking care of our users and providing a first class experience to them at all times. Bento-Box wants to open its doors for its users, and inform them about the efforts we’re making to create and improve your experience. While our first few projects remain in production, we hope to provide our anticipating audience with information regarding our progress and the kinds of features we’re in the middle of implementing.

We hope that we can connect with our users in multiple ways, one being this blog. We’re also interested in and looking into the possibility of our staff streaming while working on projects, and answering questions and taking suggestions while doing so. We definitely have plans to open a couple of social media profiles (including twitter, instagram, etc.) with which we hope we can conveniently update our users on our progress.

Projects

Although I’d like to keep our first post short, I feel that it’s in the nature of a tech blog to describe what we’re working on at bento. Bento-Box is currently focusing on our first project: idolise, and I’d like to summarize the purpose of our project without lengthening this post by too much:

idolise

is an application that aims to make staying updated on media regarding K-pop convenient, without having to make accounts and stay updated on multiple services including Twitter, Instagram, Naver/V-Live, etc. Idolise creates a news feed that combines videos, images and articles from various sources and will remove the need for you to keep tabs for each service from which you regularly receive content. Idolise also aims to provide convenience in limiting the content you receive to what you want to see, in ways such as allowing you to filter content by idol name, group name, company name, etc.
